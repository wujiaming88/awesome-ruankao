## 九、英语阅读理解（第71-75题）

**Read the following passage and answer questions 71-75.**

Cloud-native architecture represents a fundamental shift in how organizations design, build, and operate software applications. At its core, cloud-native computing leverages the advantages of the cloud computing delivery model through a set of best practices, including microservices, containers, service meshes, immutable infrastructure, and declarative APIs.

Microservices decompose applications into small, independently deployable services, each running in its own process and communicating with lightweight mechanisms, often HTTP-based REST APIs. Containers provide consistent runtime environments that package an application with its dependencies, making it portable across development, testing, and production. Kubernetes has emerged as the de facto standard for container orchestration, automating deployment, scaling, and management of containerized applications.

Service meshes, such as Istio and Linkerd, add a dedicated infrastructure layer for handling service-to-service communication. They provide critical capabilities including traffic management, security (mutual TLS), and observability without requiring changes to application code. The sidecar proxy pattern, where a proxy is deployed alongside each service instance, is the most common implementation approach.

Observability in cloud-native systems goes beyond traditional monitoring. It encompasses three pillars: metrics (quantitative measurements of system behavior), logs (timestamped records of discrete events), and traces (end-to-end request flow across distributed services). Tools like Prometheus for metrics collection, Elasticsearch for log aggregation, and Jaeger for distributed tracing form the observability stack that enables teams to understand and debug complex distributed systems.

However, adopting cloud-native architecture introduces significant operational complexity. Organizations must invest in building platform engineering capabilities, establishing GitOps workflows for continuous delivery, and cultivating a DevOps culture that bridges development and operations teams. The benefits—including faster time-to-market, improved resilience, and efficient resource utilization—justify this investment for organizations operating at scale.
