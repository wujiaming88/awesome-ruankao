## 七、英语阅读（第71-75题）

**Directions**: Read the following passage and answer Questions 71-75.

Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities across a wide range of natural language processing tasks. However, they face several fundamental challenges. One significant issue is the **hallucination problem**, where models generate plausible-sounding but factually incorrect information. This is particularly concerning in domains such as healthcare and legal advice, where accuracy is paramount.

To address this, researchers have developed **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)**, which combines the generative power of LLMs with external knowledge retrieval. In a RAG pipeline, when a user submits a query, the system first retrieves relevant documents from a knowledge base using vector similarity search, then feeds these documents as context to the LLM for generating a grounded response. This approach significantly reduces hallucinations by anchoring the model's output in verified information.

Another major concern is **alignment** — ensuring that AI systems behave in accordance with human values and intentions. Techniques such as Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) and Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) have been developed to fine-tune models so they produce outputs that are helpful, harmless, and honest. Red teaming exercises, where dedicated teams attempt to elicit harmful or undesirable outputs, have become standard practice in evaluating model safety.

The **efficiency** of LLM inference is also a critical research area. As models scale to hundreds of billions of parameters, serving them in production requires techniques such as model quantization (reducing numerical precision from FP32 to INT8 or INT4), knowledge distillation (training smaller student models to mimic larger teacher models), and pruning (removing redundant parameters). These methods enable deployment on resource-constrained devices while maintaining acceptable performance levels.
