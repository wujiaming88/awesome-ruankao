## 英语阅读（第71-75题）

**Read the following passage and answer questions 71-75.**

Software architecture is the fundamental organization of a system, embodied in its components, their relationships to each other and to the environment, and the principles guiding its design and evolution. An architecture description is a formal representation of a system's architecture. It identifies and addresses concerns from multiple stakeholders through the use of architectural views.

The concept of "architectural views" was introduced to manage the complexity inherent in large software systems. Each view addresses a specific set of concerns and is represented by a particular viewpoint. The most widely known approach is the "4+1" view model proposed by Philippe Kruchten, which consists of the Logical View, the Development View, the Process View, the Physical View, and the Scenarios (or Use Case View) that tie them together.

A key challenge in software architecture is achieving the desired quality attributes while balancing competing concerns. Quality attributes such as performance, security, availability, and modifiability are often in tension with each other. For example, adding encryption for security may degrade system performance. Architecture evaluation methods, such as ATAM (Architecture Tradeoff Analysis Method), help architects systematically identify and analyze these trade-offs before the system is built.

Modern architectures have evolved significantly. The rise of cloud computing and microservices has shifted the focus from monolithic designs to distributed systems that can scale independently. Service mesh, event-driven architecture, and serverless computing represent the latest trends in addressing scalability, resilience, and operational efficiency in complex distributed environments.
